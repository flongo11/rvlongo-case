***** Mischief Over $5000 ***** Location: 2948 St Clair Ave. City of
Windsor Charged: Francesco LONGO, a male born 1972-04-24 Charge:
Mischief over $5000 CC 430(1)(a) Damaged: Wet cement poured into brand
new pool water lines. Lines are routed from the pump to 3 separate lines
(fountain, filter and jet). All lines are full of cement. Estimated
damage over $5000. Water lines were then cut at the source to prevent
proper flushing. The fountain, filter and jet completely damaged. The
cement used to cause the damage will need to be torn up to access the
lines and re-poured. Vehicle: 2010 White Dodge Caravan Plate: CRXZ899
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
Victim 1: Hayley ZVANIGA (Hayley), a female born Victim 2: Steve ZVANIGA
(Steve), a male born
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
Narrative: On Thursday, May 6th 2021 at approximately 1254hrs, I
(Constable GRATTON) was dispatched to 2948 St Clair Ave. responding to a
report of property damage with suspect. Information received on route
indicated that the complainant, Hayley ZVANIGA, a female born had a
civil dispute with a contractor later identified as Francesco LONGO a
male born 1972-04-24. Further information received was that LONGO
arrived today, poured something into the pool filter and then cut the
water lines. Additional information was that the victim has surveillance
and LONGO stated "you messed with the wrong guy". I arrived at 1306hrs
and met with the victims, Steve and Hayley. Steve began to provide
insight into the civil dispute with LONGO. He advised that they began
digging the pool in March 2021. Ever since the beginning there have been
issues including LONGO destroying a shared fence and the neighbors yard
without their permission, See case #21-28310.

It was agreed upon that Steve would supply the pool and all equipment
required. LONGO would install the pool and do the cement around the
pool. Their agreed price was $22,600. As of today's date, Steve and
Hayley have paid in excess of $23,000 for cement and pool installation.
The pool is approximately 70-80% complete. There were several issues
with the cement pour including an instance where the backhoe collapsed 4
of the pool wall panels. As compensation, LONGO agreed to fix the panels
and supply them with the cost of the fountain. Last night, Steve advised
that he spoke and texted LONGO regarding the bill for a second
additional pour of cement. The agreed price was $5000 to pour the side
of the house. However, last night they began arguing back and forth
about the price. During that conversation, Steve advised LONGO that he
no longer wished to have his services and advised him that he would have
someone else finish the pool. They agreed to meet at 1800hrs today for
LONGO to pick up the remaining tools and agreed to pursue the matter in
civil court. Today, at approximately 1030hrs, one of LONGO's workers
arrived to pick up the mixer. At approximately 1204hrs, Hayley advised
that she observed LONGO arrive in his older model white caravan. Hayley
watched him from inside the home via her home surveillance system as she
was fearful of any retaliation. At this time, she observes LONGO
carrying a large blue bucket into her backyard where he proceeded to the
pools water pump. LONGO then leaves the backyard and returns with a
sawzall. Hayley advises she heard the sound of the sawzall cutting
something and then LONGO leaving with an empty bucket and the sawzall.

lines which were cut at the base of the cement. I observed 3 pool lines
coming from the water pump to the fountain, filter and jet. I observed
there to be cement already hardening inside the water lines. I also
observed Steve attempting to clean out the cement by a power washer with
minimal success as it has already began drying. I believe that the lines
were deliberately cut to prevent Steve from cleaning out the lines. I
was then showed video surveillance of the event. At 1204hrs, LONGO
enters the backyard with what appears to be a heavy bucket as his
posture was leaning towards his left. He proceeds to the pump and then
returns with the sawzall. I can hear the audible sound of a sawzall
cutting through something before LONGO then departs the backyard. Due to
the totality of the evidence, surveillance of LONGO entering with a
heavy bucket and sawzall then leaving the area with an empty bucket and
the fact that I observed fresh wet cement in the pools water lines, I
believe that reasonable grounds exist to arrest LONGO for mischief over
$5000. The extent of the damage is amplified by the fact that the water
lines, fountain and filter are damaged beyond repair. Adding the cost to
dig up the new cement and replace it would well in excess of $5000. At
